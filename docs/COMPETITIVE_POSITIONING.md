# SOCFlow v2 Competitive Positioning (Torq / ServiceNow / XSOAR)

This note summarizes practical product positioning opportunities based on public product pages and release notes.

## Important framing
- These platforms are strong and mature.
- Position SOCFlow as a **focused, faster-to-pilot, local-AI-friendly SOC experience** rather than claiming incumbents are "bad."

## What incumbents publicly emphasize

### Torq Hyperautomation
- Emphasizes broad hyperautomation for security operations and workflow orchestration.
- Positioning opportunity for SOCFlow: lighter onboarding experience and opinionated SOC dashboard for executive storytelling.

### ServiceNow SecOps
- Emphasizes platform-wide process integration, enterprise workflows, and AI capabilities in the Now Platform.
- Positioning opportunity for SOCFlow: analyst-first UX, faster SOC proof-of-value, and lower deployment complexity for focused SOC teams.

### Cortex XSOAR
- Emphasizes mature SOAR capabilities and ongoing AI/agentic enhancements.
- Positioning opportunity for SOCFlow: transparent local copilot workflows, deterministic explanations, and easy customization.

## SOCFlow v2 differentiators to lead with
1. **Local-first AI Copilot path** (Ollama-compatible endpoint pattern for zero API-cost demos).
2. **Single-screen SOC narrative** (Detection → Copilot → UEBA → Intel → SOAR → Report).
3. **Built-in executive metrics** (automation rate, MTTR, prevented high-severity incidents).
4. **Compliance reporting export** directly from dashboard workflows.

## Suggested GTM message
> "SOCFlow is your fast SOC value accelerator: enterprise-style workflows, local AI options, and board-ready reporting in one deployable dashboard."
